### Friday Prayer and Community

  ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **CURRENT       Every Friday, our community gathers for Jumu'ah, the weekly congregational prayer and
  DRAFT**         sermon that brings Muslims together in worship, reflection, and fellowship. It is a time
                  to pause from the busyness of the week, reconnect with Allah, and gather in the company of
                  others seeking faith and spiritual renewal.\
                  \
                  Whether you have been part of Ibrahim Center for years or are joining us for the first
                  time, you are warmly welcomed. Each week features a sermon from one of our teachers or
                  guest speakers, offering timeless Islamic wisdom and practical guidance for modern life.\
                  \
                  Jumu'ah prayers are currently held in person. Please follow our social media channels for
                  updates on guest speakers, special events, and occasional livestreamed programs.\
                  \
                  Jumu'ah Prayer begins at 1:30 PM every Friday.\
                  \
                  Location: Ibrahim Center, 618 Baca Street, Houston, Texas.\
                  \
                  Parking is available on-site and along surrounding neighborhood streets. Please be mindful
                  of our neighbors and avoid blocking driveways or restricted areas.\
                  \
                  Families and children are warmly welcomed at Ibrahim Center. We strive to cultivate an
                  environment where young people develop a love for the masjid while parents and families
                  find community, support, and belonging.\
                  \
                  Our Friday gatherings feature a rotating group of scholars, teachers, and community
                  leaders who offer sermons and reflections grounded in the Islamic tradition and relevant
                  to modern life.

|               | means of caring for members of our community who are          |
|               | experiencing financial hardship.                              |
|               |                                                               |
|               | The Qur\'an identifies several categories of individuals who  |
|               | may be eligible to receive zakat, including those facing      |
|               | poverty, debt, financial hardship, and other circumstances of |
|               | need. Through zakat, Muslims help strengthen the bonds of     |
|               | compassion, dignity, and mutual support within the community. |
|               |                                                               |
