## Our Values

*Drafted in the house voice — read and correct. Definitions pending Shaykh Khalis's review.*

**Suhbah — sacred companionship.** In the Islamic tradition, we grow in faith not alone but in the company of others who remind us of God and walk beside us toward Him. At Ibrahim Center, suhbah is the conversation that continues after prayer, the coffee shared on a Friday, and the friend who notices when you have been away.

**Ihsan — spiritual excellence.** The Prophet ﷺ described ihsan as worshipping God as though you see Him. It is the pursuit of sincerity and beauty in everything we do — our worship, our teaching, our hospitality. We try to do things well and to do them with heart, whether it is a class, a meal, or the welcome we offer a guest.

**Love.** Love of God, love of the Prophet ﷺ, and love for one another are the ground we stand on. It shows in how families are welcomed, how newcomers are received, and how we choose, again and again, to treat one another with gentleness.

**Service.** Faith matures into action. We serve one another and our neighbors through care, generosity, and showing up — from community meals and children's programs to quiet, dignified support for families in need.

## Our Culture

*Drafted in the house voice — read and correct.*

Walk into Ibrahim Center and you should feel welcomed before you are asked anything. Hospitality is not an event here; it is a habit. Whether you have prayed with us for years or are stepping into a masjid for the first time, you belong in the room.

We are a family-centered community. Children are not an interruption to the sacred — they are part of it, learning to love the masjid by being loved inside it, while parents find companionship, support, and a place to belong.

We hold to adab — the sacred etiquette of how we treat God, the space, and each other — not as a set of rules to police, but as a way of carrying ourselves with respect and care. And we are, by our founding and by our choosing, a diverse community: people of many backgrounds, generations, and stages of faith, growing together.

## Community Commitments

*Drafted in the house voice — read and correct.*

- Come as you are.
- Honor the sacredness of the space.
- Practice adab with God, the space, and one another.
- Serve one another.
- Welcome families and children.
- Make space for newcomers.
- Treat every question with care.
- Build this community intentionally, together.
