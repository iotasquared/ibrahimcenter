# Zakat Assistance

## What is Zakat?

Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam and serves as an obligation upon eligible Muslims to give a portion of their wealth to those in need. It is both an act of worship and a means of caring for members of our community who are experiencing financial hardship.

The Qur'an identifies several categories of individuals who may be eligible to receive zakat, including those facing poverty, debt, financial hardship, and other circumstances of need. Through zakat, Muslims help strengthen the bonds of compassion, dignity, and mutual support within the community.

At Ibrahim Center, we view zakat as a sacred trust. Every request is approached with care, confidentiality, and a commitment to preserving the dignity of those seeking assistance.

## Applying for Assistance

Individuals and families experiencing financial hardship are welcome to submit a zakat assistance request. Requests may include assistance for housing, utilities, food, transportation, medical expenses, emergency needs, or other qualifying circumstances. All applications are reviewed confidentially by our Zakat Committee.

## What to Expect

Our Zakat Committee meets twice each month, on the 1st and the 15th, to review submitted applications. Applicants can generally expect to receive a response following the committee's review. Depending on the nature of the request, additional information or documentation may be requested.

While assistance amounts vary based on available funds and individual circumstances, most awards provide partial financial assistance intended to help address immediate needs and provide support during difficult circumstances. Requests exceeding $5,000 are uncommon and are reviewed on a case-by-case basis.

## Our Approach

Our goal is not simply to distribute funds, but to care for members of our community with compassion, discretion, and dignity. Every situation is unique, and our committee seeks to balance individual needs, available resources, and our responsibility to the broader community.

## Confidentiality

All information submitted through the zakat application process is treated confidentially and reviewed only by members of the Zakat Committee involved in the assessment process.
