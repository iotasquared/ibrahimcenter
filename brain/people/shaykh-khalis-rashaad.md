# Biography

Shaykh Khalis Rashaad serves as the Imam and Resident Scholar of Ibrahim Center, where he provides spiritual guidance, teaches sacred knowledge, and helps cultivate a community rooted in faith, companionship, and service. Through his leadership, Ibrahim Center has become a space where traditional Islamic learning, spiritual growth, and meaningful relationships come together.

His areas of study include Islamic theology (aqidah), jurisprudence (fiqh), spirituality (tasawwuf), Qur'anic exegesis (tafsir), and applied theology. He holds ijazat in jurisprudence, hadith, theology, and spirituality, and continues advanced doctoral research in Islamic jurisprudence with a focus on the practical application of Islamic law.

As the founder and lead instructor of the Dawam Seminary for Islamic Studies, Shaykh Khalis teaches students to engage the Islamic scholarly tradition with both depth and clarity. His teaching emphasizes applied Islamic knowledge, helping Muslims navigate contemporary questions and modern life through the framework of Islamic law, theology, and spirituality.

A significant focus of his work centers on the realities facing Muslims living in the West. Through his classes, lectures, and community programs, he addresses questions surrounding identity, family, purpose, faith, ethics, and spiritual well-being.

Shaykh Khalis also leads Menaissance, Ibrahim Center's men's initiative dedicated to reviving healthy masculinity, spiritual responsibility, and sacred brotherhood. Beyond the masjid, he has spent years engaged in community outreach, mentorship, and educational initiatives.

In addition to his scholarly pursuits, Shaykh Khalis has a longstanding background in martial arts, boxing, and kickboxing. Those who know him often speak of his warmth, hospitality, and genuine interest in people. He believes sacred knowledge is best transmitted through sincere human relationships.

Shaykh Khalis also runs Ihsan Hub, an online digital learning space dedicated to bringing traditional Islamic learning into modern life.
