# Dawām Seminary

The Arabic word Dawām (دوام) means continuity, constancy, and steadfastness. The name reflects the seminary's commitment to cultivating a lifelong relationship with sacred knowledge, spiritual growth, and the continuous pursuit of God through learning and practice.

## Mission

Dawām Seminary exists to revive the Islamic scholarly tradition for the modern believer. Rooted in classical scholarship and adapted to contemporary life, the seminary seeks to cultivate spiritually grounded, intellectually confident, and ethically responsible Muslims who can faithfully engage both their tradition and the realities of modern society. The seminary combines sacred knowledge, spiritual refinement, critical thinking, and practical application to help students develop a lived and meaningful relationship with Islam.

## Study

Students study the core Islamic sciences — aqidah (theology), fiqh (jurisprudence), tafsir (Qur'anic exegesis), hadith, tazkiyah (spiritual purification), adab (sacred etiquette), applied theology, contemporary Muslim life, and ethics — with emphasis on both traditional scholarship and practical application.

## Teachers

The seminary is led by Shaykh Khalis Rashaad, Imam and Spiritual Director of Ibrahim Center, who has served the Muslim community in a teaching capacity for more than thirty years and holds ijazat in jurisprudence, hadith, theology, and spirituality. Additional instructors, scholars, and guest teachers contribute throughout the program.

## Enrollment

Designed for working adults and busy schedules. No prior seminary education is required — the program is intended for serious students who desire a structured and accessible approach to sacred knowledge. Tuition information is available during registration; scholarship opportunities and financial assistance are available for qualifying students. Register at dawamseminary.org.
