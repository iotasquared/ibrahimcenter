# Our Story (workbook §5.2 — awaiting the founder interviews)

The through-line already approved elsewhere: founded by converts who sought not simply a place to pray, but a community in which Islam could be learned, lived, and loved; grown from a small gathering rooted in faith, friendship, and sacred companionship into a 20+ year cornerstone of Houston's Third Ward, now moving into a new campus at 618 Baca Street.

The full telling — year, founders, why the name Ibrahim, the Third Ward chapters, the milestones, the future — waits on the interview answers in the verify list. Site layout guidance (workbook): timeline or alternating story-and-photo; don't overload the first screen.

Note for the eventual telling: the name Ibrahim carries the institution's method in miniature — the prophet the Qur'an calls hanif, who looked at the setting stars and testified only to what endures. A community founded by converts is a community founded on exactly that act of direct witness.
